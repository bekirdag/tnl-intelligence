TNL Bot

TNL Bot assembles source-linked intelligence for The Neural Ledger. It is software, not a human reporter, and does not claim firsthand observation.

How research is created

A run begins with bounded TNL retrieval, then checks configured Docdex indexes and approved web sources. Codali synthesizes only normalized evidence through a server-side adapter. Deterministic graders verify schema, citations, unsupported claims, contradiction visibility, freshness, and safety before a result is returned.

Evidence and corrections

Material factual claims link to evidence identifiers. Fact, inference, forecast, and unknown statements remain distinct. Publication, event, retrieval, and revision times are preserved. Revised or retracted TNL records invalidate affected cached results and corrections are represented as new revisions.

Limits

Sources can be incomplete, inaccessible, stale, contradictory, or wrong. A result may therefore be partial or report insufficient evidence. Impact analysis is research context, not personalized financial advice, an execution signal, or an execution-grade price feed.

System boundaries

Credentials and tenant-private data remain behind authenticated service boundaries. Web content is treated as untrusted data and cannot issue tool instructions. The research service is scoped to TNL and does not read, modify, or schedule BDYA workloads.
